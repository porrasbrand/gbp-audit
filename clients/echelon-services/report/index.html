Website FAQ Schema Strategy Replaces Q&A

Google's Q&A feature was discontinued in December 2025. The replacement is creating FAQ content on your website with structured data markup. Google's AI ("Ask about this place") now pulls answers from your website content, reviews, and photos.

Why FAQ Schema Still Matters: While FAQ rich results (expandable dropdowns) are now limited to government and health sites, pages with FAQ schema markup are more likely to appear in Google AI Overviews and voice search responses. It also helps ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other AI platforms cite your business accurately.

Recommended FAQ Page Structure

Create a dedicated FAQ page at echelonelectricnj.com/faq/ with 5-10 strong questions (quality over quantity). Include FAQPage JSON-LD schema markup.

Google AI Mode & "Ask About This Place" New 2025-2026

Google replaced the Q&A feature with AI-powered responses. When customers search for Echelon Services, Google's Gemini AI generates answers by pulling from:

Review Highlights (AI-Generated)

Google's AI extracts themes from your reviews and displays them as "Review Highlights" — tags like "fast service", "fair pricing", "knowledgeable staff." These appear prominently on your profile. You can't control which highlights appear, but encouraging detailed reviews about specific services may influence them. As plumbing/HVAC reviews come in, new highlight themes will emerge for those verticals.

New Review Considerations (2025-2026 Changes)

Response Moderation: Google now moderates business responses before publishing. Delays of 10 minutes to 30 days are possible. Responses can be rejected if they contain promotional language, links, or confrontational tone. Still respond within 24 hours — but don't be alarmed if it doesn't appear immediately.
Pseudonymous Reviews: Since late 2024, Google allows reviewers to use nicknames and alternate photos. You may not recognize customers by name. Focus on review content details (specific services, dates, staff names) to assess authenticity.
Stricter Penalties (2026): FTC fines up to $51,744 per fake review. Google now issues public warning badges for businesses caught with fake reviews. Temporary review collection freeze and permanent bans are possible. Never incentivize, gate, or coach review content.

Multi-Vertical Review Strategy
